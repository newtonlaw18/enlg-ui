Product/All-Rounder

How do we select ingredients for our products?

We primarily select our ingredients based on clinical effectiveness. This means that there is adequate research literature that supports the beneficial effects of the active ingredient.

Will the All-Rounder clog my pores?

It is often believed that “pore-clogging” is caused by certain emollients. In our All-Rounder, we use a blend of silicones and squalane as emollients. These ingredients are very unlikely to clog pores.

There are three main reasons for this. Firstly, silicones are well-tolerated by the human skin and are often used in healthcare settings such as wound dressings. Also, squalane is an oxidation-proof version of squalene, a natural component of human sebum. This results in a skin-friendly emollient that won’t oxidise and clog pores. Both squalane and the silicone blend have relatively large molecular sizes, making it very unlikely to penetrate pores.

It is worth noting however that the skin varies across individuals and that breakouts can be caused by various factors such as sleep, hormones and climate.

How will the All-Rounder ease issues related to sensitive skin?

The All-Rounder for dehydrated skin contains several ingredients that can ease issues related to sensitive prone skin. These are Allantoin, Panthenol and Tocopheryl Acetate.

Allantoin is an FDA-approved skin protectant. It is often used for its soothing and restoring properties.

Would a 10% Niacinamide serum be more effective than the All-Rounder for dehydrated skin which only contains 5%?

No. Beauty brands often give the impression that formulas with higher percentages of actives are always better. This is definitely not the case. Rather, what matters is the effective concentration.

We decided to go for 5% Niacinamide due to product stability issues and certain reports of sensitivity when using niacinamide at higher concentrations. Studies evaluating the skin benefits of niacinamide have shown that it is effective as low as 2%.

To compensate for this, we’ve paired it with 2% Alpha-arbutin and a range of soothing actives such as Panthenol and Allantoin. This would result in a brightening formula that is not likely to irritate the skin.

Can I use the All-Rounder for blemish-prone skin everyday?

Yes, the amount of AHAs and BHAs in the formulation are generally suitable for everyday use except those with sensitive-prone skin.

Why does the All-Rounder for blemish-prone skin sting when I apply it?

Tingling sensation is normal when using chemical exfoliants such as this All-Rounder variant. This is because the All-Rounder has a pH of 3.7 which is slightly lower than your skin’s pH.

We set the formula at this pH to produce enough free acids to work on the skin with minimal chance of irritation.

The tingling sensation should gradually subside with continuous use.

Although very rare, it is possible to experience prolonged stinging after applying. In that case, rinse immediately with water and take a break for a few days before using it again. If it still stings, please reach out to us at hello@enlg.co

Can I use the All-Rounder alongside other skincare products?

Although the All-Rounder is designed to be used as a standalone PM skin treatment after cleansing, it can be used alongside other skincare products.

If you suffer from the dry, “skin-peeling” effects as a result of using high-strength retinoids, the All-Rounder is the solution to counteract this side effect. You can apply the retinoid after leaving the All-Rounder on your face for 20 minutes.

If you are using it in the morning, make sure to use a good sunscreen after applying the All-Rounder. Check out our sunscreen recommendations that go really well with the All-Rounder!

Does the All-Rounder contain ingredients from a petrochemical source?

Yes, some of our ingredients (e.g. propylene glycol) are derived from petroleum.

However, these ingredients are petroleum byproducts. They are obtained from waste as a result of extracting petroleum for fuel. This means that even if we don't use them, they would be discarded as industrial waste.

The use of petroleum byproducts in skincare only becomes a significant environmental concern if everyone stops using petroleum for transportation.

Do enlg products contain Phenoxyethanol?

Yes, we use phenoxyethanol in our formulations due to its robustness across different applications.

Phenoxyethanol is a highly effective, versatile preservative and is safe for use according to EU/ASEAN cosmetic regulations.

Do enlg products contain the preservatives Methylchloroisothiazolinone (MCIT) and Methylisothiazolinone (MIT)?

Our current range of products do not contain MIT or MCIT.

Methylchloroisothiazolinone (MCIT) and Methylisothiazolinone (MIT) are two commonly used preservatives in personal care products. That means it can stop harmful microbes from in your skincare. They are considered effective as they can be used at very low concentrations and are stable in even the most difficult to preserve formulations. Under EU cosmetic regulations, both ingredients continue to be recommended as safe for use in rinse-off products.

A number of our rinse-off products contain a blend of both ingredients, at levels well below the recommended limits set by regulatory agencies globally. If these products are used as directed, the preservative is in contact with the skin only for a limited time and the risk of skin sensitisation is therefore very low.

Regarding ongoing usage, we would emphasise that we consistently undertake systematic reviews of all existing products to ensure that our formulas are always as safe, robust and effective as possible.

Our decision to reformulate our products has not been made lightly. The process is challenging for any product, and particularly complex when working with preservative systems. However, we believe there is an opportunity to improve the performance and quality of our products by replacing MIT and MCIT.

Do enlg products contain preservatives?

Definitely! Our products are water-based and so preservatives are required in order to prevent harmful microbes from growing inside your skincare. Any personal care product that is both water-based and preservative-free is unsafe for use.

Does the All-Rounder Cleanser contain sulphates?

No, it doesn't contain sulphates. This is mainly because sulphates can be irritating for certain types of sensitive skin.

This does not mean that sulphates are bad and must be avoided. In fact, they are so great at cleansing as surfactants to the point that they can become harsh for some.

Therefore, in order to achieve the same cleansing power of sulphates without the irritation, we use a blend of various gentle surfactants instead.

Do your products contain fragrance?

No, our products are currently fragrance-free.

There is nothing wrong with fragrance, except it can be irritating for some with sensitive skin issues. That's why we've decided to stick with fragrance-free for now so that our products are accessible to everyone!

Are enlg products phototoxic?

Nope. We don't use any citrus oils in our formulations.

Several citrus extracts can have a direct phototoxic effect on the skin, resulting in skin discolouration when in direct contact with UV light.

This is caused by a component of some essential oils called furanocoumarins, which are present in Bergamot, Lemon and other citrus oils.

Do enlg products contain parabens?

At enlg, we follow the safety regulations proposed by the Cosmetics Ingredient Review (CIR) and the Scientific Committee on Consumer Safety (SCSS). These agencies regularly review all studies on the endocrine activation, developmental and reproductive toxicity of parabens. Based on their findings, they recommend a safe concentration based on these reports.

There are several parabens authorised for use in cosmetics. We only use one type of paraben (methylparaben) in the All-Rounder for dehydrated skin at 0.%.

How does enlg test its products?

During their development, Aesop products undergo a series of rigorous and stringent tests to evaluate their safety, stability and efficacy. These tests are repeated multiple times in order to fully validate each formulation prior to launch.

All products are assessed for safety during our product development process using cumulative irritancy testing on human volunteers.

Before any product is released to the public, the formulation must pass a Product Safety Assessment and Toxicological review, conducted by a qualified independent agency. This involves the consideration of a formula both as a whole and in terms of its individual ingredients. These are complex, detailed and thorough assessments based on an assessor’s experiential knowledge as well as industry standards.

Neither our products nor the ingredients they contain have been tested on animals.

Do your products contain Acrylates Copolymer?

Acrylates Copolymer is a general title given to a family of ingredients, all with very different properties. The form we utilise is a copolymer of two or more monomers consisting of acrylic acid, methacrylic acid or one of their simple esters, and should not be confused with the plastics used to make plastic beads.

The Acrylates Copolymer used in these products is biodegradable according to European Commission Directives; and toxicological tests conducted by the manufacturer of this ingredient demonstrate that it poses no risk to aquatic organisms.

Are enlg products organic or natural?

No, being natural or organic is not part of our formulation philosophy. This is because such terms are purely conceptual and do not mean that they are safer, better or sustainable than their opposites.

However, some of the ingredients that we use such as shea butter and squalane are naturally-derived.
